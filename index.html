Case study / 01Brand / Language / Packaging / Digital

MIUNĀE

Eastern European beauty — made in RomaniaScroll to enter
The brief

Build a beauty brand with nothing borrowed.

MIUNĀE began with Romanian heritage, a grandmother’s remedies and a refusal to accept diluted skincare. Our job was to turn that truth into a complete brand — from strategy and name to bottles, words, movement and launch.

Opening frame

The world in motion.

The same tension runs through every touchpoint: clean and controlled on the surface, living underneath.

The organizing idea

Nature has its own clock.
We built the brand
around time.

The system

One truth.
Many forms.

The work needed range without losing its center. Every decision came back to time, potency, tactility and the confidence of Eastern European beauty.

Identity

An hourglass lives inside the wordmark — not as decoration, but as the core idea made visible.

Packaging

Frosted glass, deep botanical color and exact typography make the products feel collected rather than manufactured.

Language

Direct, intelligent and emotionally specific. No soft-focus beauty filler.

Imagery

Clean architecture meets overgrown nature. Hard light, true color, soil, petals and living material.

Digital

The website behaves like the brand speaks: confident, tactile and layered.

Launch

Social, films, product pages, print and education arrived from one shared point of view.

Language system / Linda

Copy as
brand behavior.

The voice was not written after the identity. It was developed beside it — using the same tension between precision and instinct, luxury and bluntness, heritage and the present.

01 / The tensionFoundation

Natural, never nostalgic.

The formulas come from Romanian knowledge and living material, but the language never performs folklore. Heritage is the source of authority, not a costume.

02 / The voicePersonality

Direct. Intelligent.
Sensory.

Confident enough to be blunt, informed enough to earn its claims and emotional enough to make texture, ritual and time feel immediate.

03 / RefusalsGuardrails

What the brand
would never say.

  • Generic wellness language
  • Borrowed mysticism
  • Soft-focus beauty filler
  • Science used as theater
04 / Language principlesSystem

Every line has
a job.

  • Say the actual thing before decorating it.
  • Let imagery carry poetry; let copy carry meaning.
  • Use short sentences when confidence matters.
  • Make education feel like part of the brand, not a disclaimer beneath it.
05 / Selected linesSpecimens

The voice,
in use.

100% natural. No water. Layered and alive.
Respectfully, right on time.
Time given. Time honored. Time refined.
06 / How it scalesApplication

One personality.
Different pressures.

The same voice becomes concise on packaging, explanatory on product pages, conversational on social and emotionally exact in campaigns — without turning into four different brands.

PackagingDigitalSocialEducation

Words with
a spine.

“You’re gonna wanna feel this.”

Linda built a voice that carries Nadine’s energy without turning it into theater: blunt where beauty copy is usually vague, sensory without becoming ornamental and informed without sounding clinical. Marian built the visual world to hold the same tension — elegant, exact and slightly feral.

End of case study / MIUNĀE

AIMS

AI music search for professional catalogsScroll to enter
The brief

Make technical depth feel immediate.

AIMS needed a clearer digital story and a more cohesive visual system — one that could explain a sophisticated product without making the experience feel dense, overworked or generic.

Opening frame

Clarity in motion.

The motion piece leads both the homepage and the case study, setting the tone before the longer product story begins.

The system

Long pages.
Light handling.

The website screens are intentionally framed as horizontal compositions. The shared AIMS background holds the set together while each page sits above it with equal breathing room.

Language system / Linda

Technical depth,
human speed.

AIMS needed to sound as intelligent as the product without asking the audience to decode the intelligence first. The message architecture leads with use, then reveals complexity only when it becomes useful.

01 / The tensionFoundation

Advanced technology.
Immediate value.

The product is sophisticated. The language should not make the user work to prove it. Complexity lives under the surface; utility arrives first.

02 / The voicePersonality

Informed, calm
and decisive.

No breathless AI language. No future-tense promises. The voice explains what the product lets people do now, with specificity and control.

03 / RefusalsGuardrails

What stayed
out.

  • AI hype and vague superlatives
  • Technical theater
  • Long explanations before the benefit
  • Category language that could belong to any platform
04 / Message architectureSystem

One question
per frame.

  • Lead with the task the user is trying to complete.
  • Move from outcome to method, never the reverse.
  • Use concrete verbs and visible examples.
  • Let interface and language share the explanatory load.
05 / Working languageSpecimens

Fast to read.

Search by what the music feels like.
Less browsing. Better matches.
06 / How it scalesApplication

A single logic
across the funnel.

The homepage establishes the promise, product pages prove it, use cases make it concrete and social content reduces the system into fast, repeatable ideas.
