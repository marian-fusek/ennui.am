Case study / 01Brand / Language / Packaging / Digital

MIUNĀE

Eastern European beauty — made in RomaniaScroll to enter
The brief

Build a beauty brand with nothing borrowed.

MIUNĀE began with Romanian heritage, a grandmother’s remedies and a refusal to accept diluted skincare. Our job was to turn that truth into a complete brand — from strategy and name to bottles, words, movement and launch.

Opening frame

The world in motion.

The same tension runs through every touchpoint: clean and controlled on the surface, living underneath.

The organizing idea

Nature has its own clock.
We built the brand
around time.

The system

One truth.
Many forms.

The work needed range without losing its center. Every decision came back to time, potency, tactility and the confidence of Eastern European beauty.

Identity

An hourglass lives inside the wordmark — not as decoration, but as the core idea made visible.

Packaging

Frosted glass, deep botanical color and exact typography make the products feel collected rather than manufactured.

Language

Direct, intelligent and emotionally specific. No soft-focus beauty filler.

Imagery

Clean architecture meets overgrown nature. Hard light, true color, soil, petals and living material.

Digital

The website behaves like the brand speaks: confident, tactile and layered.

Launch

Social, films, product pages, print and education arrived from one shared point of view.

Words with
a spine.

“You’re gonna wanna feel this.”

Linda built a voice that carries Nadine’s energy without turning it into theater: blunt where beauty copy is usually vague, sensory without becoming ornamental and informed without sounding clinical. Marian built the visual world to hold the same tension — elegant, exact and slightly feral.

Copy approach / Linda

Language built to feel potent.

Not beauty fluff. Not faux clinical jargon. The copy had to sound informed, sensual and certain — the way the products themselves behave.

01 / The tension

Heritage, not nostalgia.

The voice keeps the emotional memory of traditional remedies, but refuses any folkloric softness or sentimental fog.

02 / Voice pillars

Direct. Sensory. Intelligent.

Every line had to feel physically grounded, emotionally precise and sharp enough to stand apart from generic skincare copy.

03 / What we refused

No filler language.

No vague luxury clichés, no trend-bait wellness tone, no empty poetry detached from what the product actually does.

04 / Scale

One voice, many surfaces.

The same principles stretch from founder messaging to product pages, launch captions, packaging support and digital education.

“You’re gonna wanna feel this.”

A single line like this captures the aim: intimate without being soft, confident without shouting, sensorial without losing credibility.

End of case study / MIUNĀE

AIMS

AI music search for professional catalogsScroll to enter
The brief

Make technical depth feel immediate.

AIMS needed a clearer digital story and a more cohesive visual system — one that could explain a sophisticated product without making the experience feel dense, overworked or generic.

Opening frame

Clarity in motion.

The motion piece leads both the homepage and the case study, setting the tone before the longer product story begins.

The system

Long pages.
Light handling.

The website screens are intentionally framed as horizontal compositions. The shared AIMS background holds the set together while each page sits above it with equal breathing room.

Copy approach / Linda

Make the complex feel clear.

AIMS needed product language that respected the intelligence of the tool without burying users in explanation. The copy’s job was to reduce friction and keep the interface readable.

01 / Message structure

Lead with the value.

The clearest benefit appears first. Technical depth follows only when it earns its place and helps the user move forward.

02 / Tone

Calm, exact, modern.

The voice stays minimal and assured, avoiding startup hype and dense feature dumping in favor of sharp, efficient language.

03 / UI writing

Short enough to disappear.

Labels, microcopy and section framing are designed to guide behavior without competing with the interface or the product demo.

04 / System thinking

One story across surfaces.

Site pages, sales materials and social previews all inherit the same message logic so the brand stays legible wherever it appears.

Less explanation. More orientation.

The strongest product writing often feels invisible: it lowers cognitive load, sharpens the proposition and lets the technology look more sophisticated by sounding less performative.

touchpoint.

End of case study / AIMS
