MIUNĀE

Eastern European beauty — made in RomaniaScroll to enter
The brief

Build a beauty brand
with nothing borrowed.

MIUNĀE began with Romanian heritage, a grandmother’s remedies and a refusal to accept diluted skincare. Our job was to turn that truth into a complete brand — from strategy and name to bottles, words, movement and launch.

The organizing idea

Nature has its own clock.
We built the brand
around time.

The system

One truth.
Many forms.

The work needed range without losing its center. Every decision came back to time, potency, tactility and the confidence of Eastern European beauty.

Identity

An hourglass lives inside the wordmark — not as decoration, but as the core idea made visible. The system balances restraint with something raw underneath.

Packaging

Frosted glass, deep botanical color and exacting typography make the products feel collected rather than manufactured. Precious, but never precious about themselves.

Language

Direct, intelligent and emotionally specific. No soft-focus beauty filler. The voice says what it means, knows its standards and leaves the audience room to decide.

Imagery

Clean architecture meets overgrown nature. Hard light, true color, soil, petals and living material push against polished surfaces.

Digital

The website behaves like the brand speaks: confident, tactile and layered. Education is not hidden beneath marketing — it becomes part of the experience.

Launch

Every touchpoint arrived from one shared point of view: social, films, product pages, print, education and the systems needed to keep the brand coherent.

Words with
a spine.

“You’re gonna wanna feel this.”

Linda built a voice that carries Nadine’s energy without turning it into theater: blunt where beauty copy is usually vague, sensory without becoming ornamental and informed without sounding clinical. Marian built the visual world to hold the same tension — elegant, exact and slightly feral.

The result

A brand that
looks how it feels.
